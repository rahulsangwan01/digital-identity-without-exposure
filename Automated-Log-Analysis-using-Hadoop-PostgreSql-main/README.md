Digital Identity Without Exposure: A Privacy-Preserving Decentralized Approach

As digital services continue to grow, secure identity verification has become an essential requirement for online platforms such as e-governance, banking, healthcare, and digital communication systems. Traditional identity management systems are centralized, which creates major risks including identity theft, unauthorized access, privacy leakage, and large-scale data breaches.

This research paper proposes a Privacy-Preserving Decentralized Digital Identity System that enables secure authentication without exposing sensitive personal information. The proposed framework uses decentralized identifiers (DIDs), verifiable credentials (VCs), selective disclosure mechanisms, blockchain technology, and cryptographic verification methods to provide a secure and user-centric identity management solution.

The system removes dependency on centralized authorities and gives users complete control over their identity information while ensuring privacy, transparency, scalability, and trust.
